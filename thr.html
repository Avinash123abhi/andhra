Ways to Save Food

Store leftovers in airtight containers

Leftovers are a great way to reduce food waste, but they need to be stored properly to maintain freshness and quality. Using airtight containers helps to seal in freshness and prevent food from spoiling too quickly. Be sure to label containers with the date so you know when they were prepared.

Use freezer bags to store excess produce

If you find yourself with excess fruits or vegetables that you can't use up before they spoil, consider freezing them for later use. Freezer bags are an excellent option for storing produce in the freezer as they help to prevent freezer burn and maintain the quality of the food.

Plan meals and create a shopping list

One of the best ways to avoid overbuying food is to plan your meals in advance and create a shopping list based on what you need. By planning ahead, you can ensure that you only buy the items you need and avoid purchasing perishable items that may end up going to waste.

Learn proper food storage techniques

Knowing how to store different types of food properly is essential for preventing food waste. Some foods, like fruits and vegetables, are best stored in the refrigerator, while others, like bread and potatoes, are better kept in a cool, dark place. Take the time to learn about the proper storage techniques for the foods you buy to help them last longer.

Utilize leftovers creatively in new dishes

Instead of letting leftovers go to waste, try incorporating them into new dishes. Leftover vegetables can be added to soups or stir-fries, while leftover meats can be used to make sandwiches or salads. Get creative with your leftovers to create delicious meals while reducing food waste.

Donate excess non-perishable items

If you find yourself with excess non-perishable items that you won't be able to use, consider donating them to your local food bank or charity. Not only will you be helping those in need, but you'll also be reducing food waste by ensuring that the items are used before they expire.
